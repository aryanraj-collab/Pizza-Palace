The Story of Flavor

The Origins of Pizza

Pizza as we know it began in Naples, Italy, in the 18th century. Originally a dish for the poor, it was a simple flatbread adorned with tomatoes, which were brought to Europe from the Americas.


The legend goes that in 1889, pizzaiolo Raffaele Esposito created the "Pizza Margherita" for the Queen of Italy, Margherita of Savoy. He used tomatoes, mozzarella, and basil to represent the colors of the Italian flag: red, white, and green.

The Art of Cheese

Cheese making is an ancient craft. At Pizza Palace, we honor the tradition of Mozzarella di Bufala Campana.


True mozzarella is pulled by hand using the "pasta filata" (spun paste) method. The curds are heated in hot water and stretched until they are smooth and elastic. This process gives our pizza its signature melt and rich, milky flavor that commercial cheese simply cannot match.
